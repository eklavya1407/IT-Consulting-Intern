## Top 5 Professional Learnings

### 1. The Consulting Mindset
The most important thing I learned: a technically superior solution is worthless if it doesn't fit the client's budget, team, or context. Consulting is about finding the *right* solution, not the *best* solution in isolation.

### 2. Structured Communication
I learned to communicate differently based on the audience — detailed and technical for the internal team, simple and outcome-focused for clients. The same data can be presented in many ways; choosing the right way matters enormously.

### 3. Research is a Skill
Good research isn't just Googling. It means knowing what to look for, where to look, how to evaluate sources, and how to synthesize findings into a clear, actionable format. I developed a structured research approach using templates.

### 4. Documentation is Non-Negotiable
In a consulting firm, if it isn't documented, it doesn't exist. I learned to maintain clean, consistent, well-formatted documents that others can understand and build on.

### 5. Deadlines are Real
One month is short. I learned to prioritize, manage my time across multiple deliverables, and ask for help early rather than late.

## What I Would Do Differently
